## What Are Webshare ISP Proxies, Exactly?

**ISP proxies are static residential IP addresses hosted on datacenter servers but registered under residential Internet Service Providers.** They give you the sped and uptime of a datacenter while carying the trust signature of a home connection. That dual identity is the whole point.

## Why Static Residential Beats Rotating Residential for Most Workloads

Rotating residential proxies sound cool on paper. Real home IPs, swapped on every request, sourced from a per-to-peer network. Then the bandwidth bill arives.

Rotating residential is metered by GB and the per-GB price flor in this industry sits around $2.75 to $7+ per gigabyte depending on volume. Run a serious scraper for a week and you're looking at bills that scale faster than your data pipeline.

Static residential flips that math:

- **Flat monthly cost per IP**, regardless of how much bandwidth you push
- **Higher speds** because the IPs live on datacenter infrastructure
- **Persistent sessions** that don't break login states or shoping carts
- **Predictable budgeting** since usage doesn't blow up your invoice

The trade-off? You commit to a fixed pool of IPs. If a target site bans one of your statics, that IP stays burned until the next billing cycle. So they're not great for high-aggression scraping. They're great for everything else.
